üìä What is Linear Regression?

Linear regression is a statistical method that models the relationship between two variables by fitting a linear equation to observed data. In this analysis, we examine how GDP (Gross Domestic Product) changes over time for major world economies.

üéØ Understanding the Results

The Regression Equation: Shows the mathematical relationship between year and GDP. The coefficient indicates the average annual GDP growth across all countries.

R¬≤ Score (Coefficient of Determination): Measures how well the linear model fits the data:

  • R¬≤ = 1.0: Perfect fit - the model explains 100% of the variation
  • R¬≤ = 0.7-0.9: Strong relationship - good predictive power
  • R¬≤ = 0.5-0.7: Moderate relationship - reasonable predictions
  • R¬≤ < 0.5: Weak relationship - limited predictive value

üí° Key Insights

The blue dots represent actual GDP values for 15 countries over 6 years (2020-2025). The red line is our linear regression model that finds the best-fit straight line through these points.

The scatter pattern shows natural economic variations, including the impact of COVID-19 in 2020-2021. Despite these fluctuations, the linear trend reveals the overall growth trajectory of global GDP.
